# **Understanding Solar ROI for Industries**

Solar ROI refers to the comparison between your total investment in a solar project and the savings or income it generates over its lifetime. Solar installations are often seen as a big upfront cost, but the real question is whether this investment pays you back through reduced electricity bills or earnings from selling surplus power.

Once you understand Solar ROI, it becomes much easier to see how your investment translates into long term savings and financial gains.

To get a realistic estimate of your solar project’s performance, understanding two things is essential:

- ROI (Return on Investment)
- Payback Period

These indicators help measure whether your solar system will deliver the financial benefits you expect.

# **How to Calculate ROI on Solar Panels**

The ROI of solar panels is connected to their entire functioning lifespan. Since solar panels degrade slowly and work efficiently for decades, the warranty period (usually 20 to 25 years) is used as the estimated lifespan.

The basic formula to calculate ROI manually is:

**(Total Lifetime Savings – Total Investment Cost) / Total Investment Cost x 100**
