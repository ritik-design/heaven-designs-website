As solar and renewable projects continue to scale globally, Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) are becoming a critical part of the clean energy mix. Yet, one of the most overlooked parts of project development remains **selecting the right supplier and structuring the contract effectively**.

The discussion covered not only *what to do*, but also *what not to do* when navigating the complex BESS procurement landscape. Here’s a breakdown of the key takeaways.

### What You Shouldn’t Do

#### 1. Don’t Start Without a Clear Use Case
<p>Before reaching out to suppliers, be absolutely clear on **what applications your battery will serve** — frequency regulation, peak shaving, energy arbitrage, or backup.
Your **target use case defines cycling rates, lifetime expectations, and performance guarantees** — the foundation for both technical and financial feasibility.

#### 2. Don’t “Throw a Bottle into the Sea”

Avoid sending your RFP to every supplier you can find. Instead, **target a limited pool of Tier-1 or well-referenced manufacturers** with proven track records. Quality over quantity saves months of confusion and risk down the line.

#### 3. Don’t Ignore Interface Boundaries

Know exactly **what you’re procuring** — the full turnkey system, just the DC container, the inverter, or the transformer.
Poorly defined interfaces between suppliers, EPCs, and local authorities can create costly gaps or overlaps in responsibility.
A **RACI matrix** (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) is a practical way to map roles and prevent disputes later.

#### 4. Don’t Overlook Local Rules and Certifications

Fire safety, spacing regulations, and environmental standards differ by region.
Solutions that lack certifications such as **UL 9540** may face **insurance issues, higher premiums, or outright refusal** from financiers.

#### 5. Don’t Evaluate Only on Cost

Price is just one factor. Always assess **performance, references, after-sales support, warranty terms, and supplier credibility** — all of which directly impact financing confidence.

### What You Should Do

#### 1. Define Clear Performance Metrics

Your contract should specify:

- Availability rate
- Round-trip efficiency
- Usable capacity (at the point of connection)

Link these KPIs with **penalty clauses** if performance falls below the agreed thresholds.

#### 2. Clarify Scope and Timelines

Your procurement documents should spell out:

- Site conditions and grid connection details
- Targeted COD (Commercial Operation Date) and handover schedule
- Step-by-step procurement and testing process

The more precise your scope, the fewer disputes and surprises later.

#### 3. Build in Transparency

Whether public or private procurement, **transparency across bidders** encourages healthy competition and ensures fairness.
This not only attracts better offers but also strengthens your negotiation position.

#### 4. Consider Financing Early

Banks and insurers favor projects with **proven suppliers, proper certifications, and robust warranties**. Align your technical and financial teams early to avoid late-stage deal breakers.

### The Bigger Picture

Battery storage isn’t just about technology — it’s about **long-term reliability and financial performance**.
The message from Sinovoltaics’ experts was clear: success depends as much on **the contract and supplier selection** as on the engineering design.

By following these guidelines, project developers and investors can avoid expensive pitfalls, ensure compliance, and build BESS assets that deliver reliable returns for years to come.
